
## Common Medicines for Diabetes
**Description:**  
Diabetes medicines help the body lower blood sugar in different ways. Some increase insulin, others help the body use it better or reduce sugar made by the liver.

**Keywords:** metformin, insulin, tablets, medicine, diabetes treatment  

### Details:
- **Metformin:** The first-line medicine for most people with Type 2 Diabetes. It makes your body more sensitive to insulin and reduces sugar production in the liver.
- **Sulfonylureas** (e.g., Glimepiride, Gliclazide): Stimulate the pancreas to produce more insulin.
- **DPP-4 inhibitors** (e.g., Sitagliptin, Linagliptin): Help your body release insulin only when needed and prevent sudden sugar spikes after meals.
- **SGLT2 inhibitors** (e.g., Dapagliflozin, Empagliflozin): Remove extra sugar through urine; also protect the heart and kidneys.
- **Thiazolidinediones** (e.g., Pioglitazone): Improve insulin sensitivity but can cause weight gain or swelling; used with caution.
- **Insulin:** Used when oral medicines are not enough or in Type 1 Diabetes.

## Insulin Therapy
**Description:**  
Insulin is a hormone that lowers blood sugar. When the pancreas can’t make enough, insulin must be injected.

**Keywords:** insulin, injection, storage, dosage  

### Details:
- **Types of Insulin:**
  - **Rapid/Short-acting:** Taken before meals (e.g., Lispro, Aspart).
  - **Intermediate/Long-acting:** Provides all-day control (e.g., NPH, Glargine).
  - **Premixed:** Combines both for convenience.
- Insulin dose is decided by the doctor; do not adjust on your own.
- Store insulin in a refrigerator (2–8 °C); never freeze it.
- Rotate injection sites (abdomen, thighs, upper arms) to prevent lumps.

## Safe Use of Medicines
**Description:**  
To get the best results, medicines should be taken on time and as prescribed.

**Keywords:** dose, timing, missed medicine  

### Details:
- Take medicine at the same time every day.
- Follow the doctor’s advice on whether to take it before or after meals.
- If a dose is missed, skip it and continue the next scheduled dose; don’t double.
- Do not stop or change medicines without medical advice.
- Report side effects like stomach upset, dizziness, or swelling.

---

## Herbal or Home Remedies
**Description:**  
Many local or herbal products claim to cure diabetes, but most are unproven.

**Keywords:** herbal, natural, safety  

### Details:
- Some herbs may lower sugar but can react with your medicine.
- Always consult your doctor before trying any supplement.
- No herbal product can replace prescribed treatment.

---

### Example Q&A:
**Q:** What does Metformin do?  
**A:** It helps your body use insulin better and reduces sugar made by the liver.

**Q:** Can I stop insulin if my sugar becomes normal?  
**A:** Never stop insulin suddenly. Your doctor will decide if and when to reduce the dose.
