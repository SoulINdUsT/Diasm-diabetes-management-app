

**Description:**  
Regular glucose monitoring helps you understand how your food, activity, and medicines affect blood sugar.

**Keywords:** glucose, testing, fasting, post-meal, HbA1c  

### Details:
- **Fasting blood sugar:** Check in the morning before breakfast; goal < 7.0 mmol/L (126 mg/dL).  
- **Post-meal (2 hours after eating):** Goal < 10.0 mmol/L (180 mg/dL).  
- **HbA1c (average 3-month sugar):** Test every 3–6 months; goal < 7%.  
- Use a glucometer or continuous glucose monitor (CGM) if available.  
- Record results in your diary or app to track patterns.

## Weight, Blood Pressure, and Cholesterol
**Description:**  
These numbers affect diabetes control and heart health.

**Keywords:** weight, BMI, blood pressure, cholesterol  

### Details:
- Weigh yourself once a month; aim for a BMI between 18.5–24.9.  
- Check blood pressure at least monthly; keep below 130/80 mmHg if possible.  
- Check cholesterol yearly; high LDL increases heart-attack risk.  
- Even a 5–7 kg weight loss can improve blood sugar control.

## Foot Care
**Description:**  
Diabetes can damage nerves and reduce blood flow to the feet, leading to infections or ulcers.

**Keywords:** foot care, ulcers, infection, neuropathy  

### Details:
- Wash and dry your feet daily; inspect for cuts, redness, or swelling.  
- Don’t walk barefoot, even at home.  
- Trim nails carefully; avoid cutting corners too deep.  
- Wear soft, closed shoes and cotton socks.  
- If you find wounds or numbness, see a doctor immediately.

---

## Eye and Dental Care
**Description:**  
High sugar can damage the eyes and gums.

**Keywords:** retinopathy, eye exam, dental hygiene  

### Details:
- Have a **retina (eye-back)** exam once a year even if vision feels normal.  
- Report blurred vision, floaters, or dark spots immediately.  
- Brush twice daily and visit a dentist every 6 months to prevent gum infections.

---

## Stress, Sleep, and Routine
**Description:**  
Emotional health strongly influences sugar levels.

**Keywords:** stress, sleep, lifestyle  

### Details:
- Sleep 7–8 hours daily; late nights increase sugar and cravings.  
- Try meditation, breathing, or quiet walks to reduce stress.  
- Keep meal and exercise timing regular.

---

### Example Q&A:
**Q:** How often should I check my blood sugar?  
**A:** At least twice a week for stable diabetes, or daily if adjusting medicine. Always test fasting and 2 hours after meals.  

**Q:** How can I protect my feet?  
**A:** Keep them clean, dry, and covered; never walk barefoot; report any wound early.
