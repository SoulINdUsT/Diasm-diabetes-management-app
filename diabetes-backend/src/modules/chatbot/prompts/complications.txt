
## Eye Complications (Diabetic Retinopathy)
**Description:**  
High blood sugar damages small blood vessels in the eyes, leading to blurred vision or blindness.

**Keywords:** eyes, retinopathy, vision, blindness  

### Details:
- Symptoms: Blurred vision, difficulty seeing at night, black spots.  
- Prevention: Keep sugar, blood pressure, and cholesterol under control.  
- Screening: Eye (retina) exam once a year, even if vision seems fine.  
- Treatment: Early laser therapy or injections can prevent blindness.

## Kidney Complications (Diabetic Nephropathy)
**Description:**  
Sugar and high blood pressure damage the kidney filters, causing protein leakage in urine.

**Keywords:** kidneys, nephropathy, urine protein  

### Details:
- Signs: Swelling of legs or face, foamy urine.  
- Test urine for protein (microalbumin) yearly.  
- Reduce salt intake and maintain blood pressure below 130/80 mmHg.  
- Avoid unnecessary painkillers or herbal medicines that harm kidneys.

## Nerve Damage (Diabetic Neuropathy)
**Description:**  
Chronic high sugar injures nerves, causing numbness, tingling, or burning in the feet and hands.

**Keywords:** neuropathy, tingling, numbness, pain  

### Details:
- Common in feet; may start as mild burning or loss of sensation.  
- Increases risk of unnoticed wounds and infections.  
- Control sugar, do regular exercise, and take vitamin B complex if advised.  
- Avoid smoking and alcohol — they worsen nerve damage.

---

## Foot Problems
**Description:**  
Due to neuropathy and poor circulation, wounds heal slowly and can become serious infections.

**Keywords:** foot ulcer, gangrene, amputation  

### Details:
- Daily foot inspection is essential.  
- If any wound doesn’t heal within a week, see a doctor.  
- Use comfortable shoes and keep nails short.  
- Never use hot water bags on feet (can burn numb skin).

---

## Heart and Blood Vessel Disease
**Description:**  
People with diabetes are 2–4 times more likely to develop heart disease or stroke.

**Keywords:** heart, stroke, cholesterol, hypertension  

### Details:
- High sugar, blood pressure, and cholesterol together are dangerous.  
- Eat less oil, salt, and red meat.  
- Quit smoking and limit alcohol completely.  
- Walk daily and manage stress.

---

## Skin and Mouth Problems
**Description:**  
High sugar increases risk of infections in skin and gums.

**Keywords:** skin infection, fungal, dental  

### Details:
- Keep skin clean and dry; moisturize after bathing.  
- See a doctor if you notice itching, rash, or white patches.  
- Maintain oral hygiene; bleeding gums or tooth pain need early care.

---

## Sexual and Hormonal Issues
**Description:**  
Nerve and blood flow problems can affect sexual health.

**Keywords:** erectile dysfunction, libido, vaginal infection  

### Details:
- Men may face erection problems; women may have dryness or infections.  
- Good sugar control and stress reduction improve symptoms.  
- Don’t hesitate to discuss with a doctor — treatments exist.

---

## Emotional and Cognitive Effects
**Description:**  
Chronic diabetes can cause fatigue, anxiety, or mild memory problems.

**Keywords:** depression, stress, cognition  

### Details:
- Feeling low or tired is common but treatable.  
- Regular physical activity, meditation, and social connection help.  
- Seek help if sadness or hopelessness persists beyond two weeks.

---

### Example Q&A:
**Q:** Why do my feet feel numb?  
**A:** High sugar can damage nerves, especially in the feet. Keep sugar in control, wear protective shoes, and get your feet checked regularly.  

**Q:** How can I protect my kidneys?  
**A:** Drink enough water, limit salt, control blood pressure, and test urine protein yearly.
